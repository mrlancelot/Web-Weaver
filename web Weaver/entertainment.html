  • India Gate

    India Gate was dedicated to all the Indian soldiers of the British Army who died in World War I.At nightfall India Gate is beautifully lit up, including the surrounding fountains and makes for a beautiful sight.

  • Qutub Minar

    The tower was built to celebrate Muslim dominance in Delhi after the defeat of Delhi’s last Hindu ruler. This tower is the highest tower in India, complete with five storeys and projecting balconies.

  • Rashtrapati Bhavan

    The Rashtrapati Bhavan is the official residence of the President of India and is a rambling mansion with breath taking architecture.Situated at the eastern edge of Rajpath, the Rashtrapati Bhavan faces the India Gate, which lies at the Western edge of the same road.

  • Chandni Chowk

    Chandni Chowk remains famous for being one of Delhi’s largest wholesale markets and attracts numerous shoppers on a daily basis.The market is also a foodie’s dream come true as Chandni Chowk is famous for its street food such as chaat, paranths and dahi bhallas.

  • Lotus Temple

    The Lotus temple, constructed entirely out of white marble, is known for its modern architecture, which stands out against the concrete jungle around it.The temple is surrounded by beautifully maintained laws and the best time to visit the temple is during sunset.

  • Indira Gandhi Memorial Museum

    The once residence of late Indian Prime Minister, the Indira Gandhi Memorial Museum is now home to artifacts and interesting memorabilia related to Indira Gandhi. A few of the rooms of her former residence have been preserved.

  • Ishq-e-Delhi

    Ishq-e-Delhi is a sound and light show held at the Purana Qila (Old Fort) and is an audio-visual treat. The show traces the history of Delhi right from the time when Delhi was the ancient city of Indiraprastha.The show is in both Hindi and English.

  • Lodi Gardens

    Emblematic of 16th century architectural styles, the Lodi Gardens contain the tombs of Muhammad Shah and Sikandar Lodi.The best time to go to the Lodi Gardens is around sunset when the sunlight is mellow and the tombs are beautifully lit up.

  • Red Fort (Lal Qila)

    A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Red Fort took a total of ten years to be completed. The main gate to the Red Fort is the Lahore Gate and is also the site for the Prime Ministers speech every year on Independence Day.
